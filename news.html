Secretary General Appointment

The Missouri Fur Association is honored to announce the appointment of Mohammed Ahmed as Secretary General of the Kansas City Chapter. This milestone marks an important step in strengthening the Association’s commitment to service, cultural preservation, and community empowerment.

Mohammed brings with him a solid understanding of community needs assessment approaches, developed through years of work with grassroots organizations and government policy-making entities in both Sudan and the United States. His expertise in social work, economic planning, and research has consistently served as a catalyst for meaningful change in community dynamics.

He earned a Bachelor’s degree with honors in Agricultural Extension from Sudan University of Science and Technology, followed by a Master of Science in Economics – Development Planning from the University of Khartoum. Most recently, he achieved a Graduate Certificate in Data Analytics and Visualization from the University of Kansas, equipping him with advanced tools to integrate evidence-based insights into community initiatives.

As Secretary General, Mohammed will oversee programs, partnerships, and initiatives that advance the Association’s mission. His leadership will focus on empowering families, fostering youth engagement, and ensuring that the voices of the community remain central to the Missouri Fur Association’s work.

“This appointment is more than a personal honor—it is a call to serve,” Mohammed shared. “I look forward to working hand in hand with our community to build sustainable programs and opportunities for our future generations.”

The Missouri Fur Association welcomes Mohammed into this role with great confidence and looks forward to the positive impact his vision and leadership will bring.
